You Don't Need a Huge Budget to Start. You Need a Smart Starting Point.

Remco Vroom · Apr 26, 2026 · 7 min read

Two companies. Two AI investments. Two outcomes. Company A spent $85,000 on an AI-powered customer support system. Result: 62% of support issues now resolved without any human involvement. Company B spent $28,000 on an AI lead qualification system. Result: $4.2 million in new revenue.

Let me repeat those numbers for clarity.

$28,000 in. $4.2 million out.

Neither of these is a Fortune 500 company with a hundred-million-dollar technology transformation budget. Neither had a dedicated AI team. Neither spent two years on a strategy before they started.

They found the right starting point.

And that, more than any other factor, determined their outcome.

The Mid-Market Moment, Why Smaller Is Actually an Advantage

91% of middle market executives are already using AI in some form. Not enterprise. Not Silicon Valley. Middle market, companies between $10M and $1B in revenue.

67% of Inc. 5000 companies are actively implementing AI. These are fast-growing, resource-conscious businesses solving specific problems with specific tools.

And here is the structural advantage that smaller organizations have:

Smaller means faster. And faster is the whole game right now.

In a large enterprise, a new AI initiative requires a business case, procurement, security review, legal review, change management, pilot, evaluation framework, and a board presentation. Start to pilot: twelve to eighteen months.

In a mid-sized organization, a motivated leader with a clear use case can go from idea to working pilot in four to six weeks.

The Framework: Start Where It Hurts

Every organization has a specific process that everyone knows is broken. A workflow that eats hours no one can afford to lose. A system that causes visible friction every single week.

That is your starting point.

Not where AI is most intellectually interesting. Not where the technology is most impressive in a demo. Where the pain is most real, most frequent, and most costly.

Three questions to find it:

Where does your team waste the most time? Look at calendar audits, "this is taking forever" complaints, overtime on specific tasks.

Where does the most frustration live? Frustration points to a process designed for a different era, and that gap is where AI can close the distance.

Where are you losing the most money? Sometimes in direct costs, sometimes in missed opportunities: deals that stall, customers who churn, campaigns that underperform because there wasn't enough time to test.

Find the intersection of those three questions. That's your first project.

One process. Bounded scope. Measurable outcome. Ninety days.

The Analogy That Makes This Land

Imagine you've just bought a house.

It needs work. Substantial work. The kitchen is outdated. The bathrooms need redoing. The flooring has seen better days.

You could approach this as a full renovation. Gut the kitchen, redo the bathrooms simultaneously, replace all the flooring, build the extension, the whole vision, executed at once.

Most people who take that approach spend two years living in a construction zone, double their original budget, and end up resenting the house.

Or you could start with the room that bothers you most.

Fix that room. Do it properly. Take the time to get it right. Then step back and live in the improvement for a moment. Notice how much better the day feels when that specific friction is gone. Use that win to build the case for what comes next.

AI transformation works exactly the same way.

The companies that have done this consistently, room by room, process by process, quarter by quarter, are the ones who, three years later, look like they've done something remarkable. They didn't do anything remarkable. They did something specific, repeatedly.

Monks Success Story

At Monks, we've been thinking hard about a specific problem: how do you give every marketing organization, regardless of size, access to enterprise-grade AI capability without the complexity, cost, and implementation time?

The answer is Monks.Flow.

Henry Cowling, our CEO, describes it as "the productization layer on top of foundational models for the marketing organization."

What does that mean in plain terms? We've taken the hard part, the model selection, the prompt engineering, the integration architecture, and turned it into something accessible. A system that covers the full marketing lifecycle:

Plan, audience intelligence, strategic briefing, competitive insight generation.

Create, AI-assisted content development, adapted to brand voice and channel requirements.

Scale, asset variation, localization, personalization across markets and audiences.

Deliver, distribution logic, performance feedback loops, optimization recommendations.

The guiding principle: your data, your models, your outcomes.

Your One Action This Week

Here is your task. It takes ten minutes and requires nothing but honesty.

Write down the single biggest time-waster in your organization right now. The process that, if you're being honest with yourself, you've known for a while is broken, inefficient, or simply not fit for how your business operates today.

Now ask one question:

Could AI make this 50% faster?

Not perfect. Not fully automated. Not zero-cost. Just 50% faster.

If the answer is yes, or even "probably, I'd need to test it", that is your first project.

Not a strategy. Not a roadmap. A project. With a scope, a timeline, a cost, and a success metric.

Start there. The house gets renovated one room at a time. This is your first room.
